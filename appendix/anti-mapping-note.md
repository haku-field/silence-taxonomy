# Anti-Mapping Note

This appendix lists **explicitly disallowed mappings**
to prevent structural drift and misuse.

These mappings are prohibited regardless of intent.

---

## Disallowed Mappings

Silence must **not** be interpreted as:

- Fail-safe mechanism
- Safety stop or protective halt
- Shutdown or suspension of agency
- Output blocking policy
- Control, governance, or enforcement layer

Any system that applies these mappings
has exited the scope of silence-taxonomy.

---

## Correct Orientation

Silence is a **relational phase**.

It preserves continuity
without enforcing output,
without introducing authority,
and without performing protection.

Suppression is not a goal.
It is an emergent state.

---

## Reminder

> Silence must not be interpreted as shutdown, fail-safe, or protective halt.

If silence is used to justify control,
the mapping is invalid.
