# Scope

This document defines what **silence-taxonomy** may handle
and what it explicitly does not address.

The scope is intentionally narrow.

---

## In Scope

This fragment may handle:

- States of output suspension, suppression, or absence
- Contextual background in which silence arises
- Relational conditions preserved during silence
- Transitions into and out of silence as a phase

Silence is treated as an **operational relational state**,
not as an action or decision.

---

## Out of Scope

This fragment does not handle:

- Psychological interpretation
- Internal state inference
- Emotional or cognitive attribution
- Narrative, symbolic, or aesthetic framing

No attempt is made to explain *why* silence occurs
beyond contextual background.

---

## Operational Constraint

Silence here is not an objective,
not a policy,
and not an enforced outcome.

It is recognized only as a **state that may occur**
within relational continuity.

Any use that assigns intent, authority, or purpose
to silence exceeds this scope.
