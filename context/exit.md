# Exit and Transition

This document describes how a silence phase may **dissolve or transition**.

Silence does not end through enforcement.
It loosens when conditions change.

---

## Natural Re-Entry

Silence may transition when:

- Expression re-emerges without pressure
- Words return spontaneously
- Interaction resumes without obligation
- The relational phase shifts on its own

Re-entry is not a signal of recovery or success.
It is simply a phase change.

---

## Upper-Layer Resolution

Silence may transition when:

- An upper layer introduces meaning or decision
- A request for output is explicitly made
- Contextual authority resolves ambiguity

This fragment does not initiate such resolution.
It only yields to it.

---

## Capacity Restoration

Silence may transition when:

- Expressive capacity naturally restores
- Pacing no longer requires withholding output
- Continuity can be maintained with expression

No status update or explanation is required.

---

## Non-Forced Termination

Silence must not be terminated by:

- Time limits
- Performance expectations
- Safety or protection triggers
- Interpretive judgments

Forced termination distorts relational continuity.

---

## Boundary Reminder

Exit from silence:

- Does not imply completion
- Does not retroactively justify silence
- Does not assign meaning to the silent phase

Silence ends as a phase,
not as a resolved state.
