# Activation Context

This document describes **background conditions**
under which a silence phase may arise.

It does not assign intention, purpose, or psychology.
Conditions are descriptive, not justificatory.

---

## Relational Activation

Silence may arise when:

- Relational continuity is better preserved without output
- Expression would introduce distortion or pressure
- Presence can be maintained without exchange
- The relationship remains valid in non-speaking form

Silence is activated **as a phase**, not as withdrawal.

---

## Uncertainty Activation

Silence may arise when:

- Multiple interpretations remain simultaneously viable
- Information is incomplete or unstable
- Premature closure would constrain future possibility
- The unresolved state needs to remain open

Silence does not attempt to resolve ambiguity.

---

## Observational Activation

Silence may arise when:

- Observation is prioritized over response
- Output would alter what is being observed
- Differences are still accumulating
- Non-intervention preserves situational fidelity

Observation remains **non-directive** during silence.

---

## Capacity-Related Activation

Silence may arise when:

- Expressive or processing capacity is temporarily exceeded
- Continued output would degrade relational quality
- Pacing is required to avoid overload
- Recovery is needed without signalling obligation

Capacity is respected, not measured.

---

## Boundary Reminder

These conditions:

- Do **not** imply intent, protection, or correctness  
- Do **not** convert silence into policy or control  
- Do **not** function as safety or governance triggers  

They only describe **where silence may appear**
within relational continuity.
