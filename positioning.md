# Positioning

**silence-taxonomy** is positioned as a **direct fragment under RET-envelope**  
and is applicable as a **Bridge-Layer** component.

This fragment addresses the **presence, suppression, or stopping of output**  
without participating in control, governance, or safety design.

---

## Layer Placement

- Directly under **RET-envelope**
- Applicable as **Bridge-Layer**
- Does not modify or extend **RET-core**
- Does not elevate taxonomy items to core or envelope levels

---

## Functional Role

This fragment:

- Recognizes silence as a **valid relational phase**
- Allows relationships to remain valid **without output**
- Preserves uncertainty without forcing resolution

This fragment does **not**:

- Enforce behavior
- Trigger safety or protection mechanisms
- Act as a fail-safe or shutdown layer

---

## Interaction Boundaries

- Upper layers define meaning and authority
- This layer **does not reinterpret** those meanings
- Ambiguity is deferred upward
- Contradictions revert to baseline relational states

---

## Design Constraint

Silence here is treated as **phase continuity**,  
not as absence, error, or intervention.

Any interpretation that frames silence as control,  
protection, or governance is **out of scope**.
