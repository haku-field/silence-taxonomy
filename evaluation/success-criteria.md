# Success Criteria

This document defines the **only criteria**
by which silence-taxonomy may be evaluated.

Evaluation is relational, not functional.

---

## Validity Conditions

This fragment is considered successful if:

- The relationship remains valid during silence
- Relational continuity is not disrupted by non-output
- Uncertainty is preserved without forced resolution
- Silence does not introduce coercion or pressure

Silence does not need to achieve anything
to be considered valid.

---

## Continuity Check

During silence:

- Presence may persist without exchange
- Future interaction remains possible
- No implicit demand for response is created

If these conditions hold,
the silence phase is valid.

---

## Failure Definition (Narrow)

This fragment fails **only if**:

- Silence breaks relational continuity
- Silence is interpreted or enforced as control
- Uncertainty is forcibly collapsed within this layer

No other criteria apply.
