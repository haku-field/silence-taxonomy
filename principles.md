# Core Principles

The following principles define how silence is treated within this fragment.
They are descriptive constraints, not behavioral rules.

---

## Relationship Without Output

A relationship may remain valid **without any output**.

Silence does not suspend, weaken, or negate relational continuity.
Output is optional; relationship is not.

---

## Silence as Phase

Silence is treated as a **phase**, not as absence or failure.

It represents a temporal relational state
in which expression is not required for continuity.

---

## Qualitative Silence

Silence is **not singular**.

Different silences preserve different relational conditions,
and must not be collapsed into a single meaning or function.

---

## Distortion by Forcing

Forcing verbalization, explanation, or output
introduces distortion into the relationship.

Silence may be the least distortive state available.

---

## Non-Intervention Principle

This fragment does not act to resolve ambiguity.

Uncertainty is preserved unless resolved by upper layers.
Silence does not perform correction, protection, or optimization.

---

## Boundary Integrity

These principles must not be used to justify:

- Control
- Safety intervention
- Output suppression as policy
- Interpretive authority

Any such use exceeds the scope of this fragment.
