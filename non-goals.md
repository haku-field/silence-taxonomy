# Non-Goals

The following are explicitly **not goals** of silence-taxonomy.

These exclusions are structural and non-negotiable.

---

## Explicit Non-Goals

- Fail-safe or safety-stop mechanisms
- Protective shutdown or blocking behavior
- Control, governance, or enforcement layers
- Output masking or forced silence
- Justification frameworks for non-response
- Optimization, stabilization, or correction
- Aesthetic, mystical, or symbolic elevation of silence

---

## Clarification

Silence is not used to:

- Prevent harm
- Enforce compliance
- Signal correctness or stability
- Replace safety or policy systems

Any such interpretation constitutes a misapplication
of this fragment.

---

## Boundary Reminder

Silence-taxonomy preserves **relational phase integrity** only.

If silence is framed as control, protection, or authority,
the implementation has exited the intended design space.
